AI for Educators

Building Digital Literacy and Ethical Practice

Course Information
Duration: 6 weeks
Total Hours: 12 hours
Format: Hybrid
Target Audience: K-12 educators
Experience Level: All levels
Subject Areas: Cross-curricular
Course Description

This comprehensive course introduces K-12 educators to artificial intelligence fundamentals, practical classroom applications, and ethical considerations. Participants will develop AI literacy, explore educational tools, and create strategies for responsible AI integration across diverse subject areas and grade levels.

Differentiated Learning Approach

This course is designed to accommodate educators with varying technology experience levels:

  • Beginners: Focus on concrete examples and basic vocabulary
  • Intermediate: Explore practical applications and implementation strategies
  • Advanced: Dive into technical concepts and leadership opportunities

Learning Objectives

What you'll accomplish by the end of this course

By Course Completion, You Will Be Able To:
  • Understand fundamental AI concepts and their educational implications
  • Evaluate AI tools critically for classroom appropriateness and effectiveness
  • Implement ethical AI practices and teach AI literacy to students
  • Design subject-specific activities incorporating AI tools responsibly
  • Develop policies and procedures for AI use in your educational context
  • Model digital citizenship and critical thinking about AI for students

Week 1: AI Fundamentals

What Every Educator Needs to Know

Week 1
Essential Questions: What is AI? How does it impact education?
Topics Covered
  • Demystifying AI: Basic definitions and concepts (machine learning, algorithms, data)
  • AI in daily life: Examples students already encounter
  • Educational landscape: How AI is changing teaching and learning
  • Myths vs. reality: Common misconceptions about AI capabilities
Activities
  • AI scavenger hunt: Identifying AI in everyday tools
  • Small group discussions: Sharing current AI experiences
  • Hands-on exploration: Testing simple AI tools (voice assistants, translation apps)
Beginners: Focus on concrete examples and basic vocabulary with guided exploration

Advanced: Explore technical concepts and help mentor other participants

Week 3: Responsible AI Use

Ethics and Best Practices

Week 3
Essential Questions: What are our responsibilities when using AI in education? How do we use AI ethically?
Key Ethical Principles
  • Transparency: Always disclose AI use to students and parents
  • Privacy: Protect student data and comply with FERPA/COPPA
  • Equity: Ensure AI tools don't exacerbate existing inequalities
  • Authenticity: Maintain academic integrity standards
  • Agency: Preserve human judgment in educational decisions
Week 3 Deliverable

Draft Classroom AI Policy Document - Create a comprehensive policy for AI use in your classroom that addresses ethical considerations, student guidelines, and implementation procedures.

Week 6: Implementation Planning

Taking Action in Your Classroom

Week 6
Essential Questions: How will I implement what I've learned? How will I continue growing in AI literacy?
Implementation Framework
  1. Start Small: Begin with one tool in one class
  2. Document: Keep records of successes and challenges
  3. Reflect: Regular evaluation of AI integration effectiveness
  4. Share: Contribute to school/district AI learning community
  5. Grow: Gradually expand successful practices
Final Projects
  • Personal AI integration plan for the coming semester
  • Resource toolkit for ongoing reference
  • Peer mentoring partnerships establishment

Course Policies
Regular attendance is essential due to the hands-on, collaborative nature of the course. Make-up sessions available for excused absences.
Participants will use various AI tools during class. All tool use is voluntary, and alternative activities are provided for those uncomfortable with specific platforms.
Student information should never be input into AI tools. Use fictional examples or anonymized data for all practice activities.
While learning about AI tools, all course assignments should represent participants' original thinking and reflection, with appropriate attribution for AI assistance.
